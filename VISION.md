# 🇳🇵 Kurra — Product Vision

Kurra is not a generic language learning app.

It is built with one clear goal:
> Help users speak Nepali confidently, as fast as possible.

---

## 🎯 Core Principles

### 1. Speak-first, always
Users should be speaking from day one.

- No long onboarding
- No heavy grammar explanations early
- No passive learning

---

### 2. Recall over recognition
We prioritise:
- active recall
- thinking and producing language

We avoid:
- multiple choice guessing
- passive tapping exercises

---

### 3. Low friction, fast progress
The product should feel:
- simple
- fast
- rewarding

Every feature must reduce friction, not add to it.

---

### 4. User choice matters

Kurra supports two paths:

- 🗣️ Speak Only → for users who want conversational fluency fast  
- ✍️ Speak + Script → for users who want full literacy  

Neither path should block the other.

---

### 5. Real-world usefulness

We prioritise:
- phrases people actually use
- cultural context
- practical understanding

We avoid:
- textbook-style content
- overly formal or unnatural phrasing

---

## 🚫 What Kurra is NOT

- Not Duolingo for Nepali
- Not a grammar-first app
- Not a feature-heavy platform
- Not designed for academic learning

---

## 🧠 Product Philosophy

Every feature should answer:

> “Does this help the user speak or understand Nepali faster?”

If the answer is no, it should not be built.

---

## 🧭 Direction

Kurra will evolve towards:
- real-time conversation practice
- AI-powered feedback
- personalised recall systems

---

## 🤝 Contributions

We welcome contributions, but maintain strong product direction.

Contributions that do not align with this vision may not be accepted.
